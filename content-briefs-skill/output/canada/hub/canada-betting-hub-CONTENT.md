## Ontario vs Non-Ontario Betting

Sports betting operates very differently depending on whether you're in Ontario or another Canadian province.

### iGaming Ontario: The Regulated Market

Ontario launched Canada's only competitive iGaming market on April 4, 2022. Two bodies oversee operations:

- **AGCO (Alcohol and Gaming Commission of Ontario):** Establishes regulatory standards
- **iGaming Ontario (iGO):** Manages commercial agreements with private operators

As of October 2025, 50 licensed operators run 88 gaming websites in Ontario. Major brands include FanDuel, BetMGM, Bet365, DraftKings, PointsBet, and theScore Bet.

**Ontario Consumer Protections:**
- Mandatory deposit limits and time limits
- Easy self-exclusion access
- RG Check accreditation required for all operators
- Fair-tested games and secure payment systems
- Advertising restrictions (no active athletes in ads since February 2024)

### Licensed vs Offshore Sites

| Factor | Ontario Licensed | Offshore (Other Provinces) |
|--------|------------------|----------------------------|
| Regulation | AGCO/iGaming Ontario | Curacao, Panama, Malta |
| Consumer Protection | Government oversight | Operator discretion |
| Dispute Resolution | Regulatory recourse | Customer service only |
| Cryptocurrency | NOT allowed | Widely accepted |
| Welcome Bonuses | Regulated, advertised | Often more generous |
| Payment Options | Standard methods | More variety including crypto |

### Sports Gambling Regulations

**Federal Framework:** Bill C-218 (The Safe and Regulated Sports Betting Act) received Royal Assent on June 22, 2021, and came into force on August 21, 2021. This amended Criminal Code Section 207 to permit single-game betting—previously only parlay bets were legal.

**Provincial Implementation:**
- **Ontario:** Open market with private operators
- **British Columbia:** BCLC's PlayNow.com only
- **Quebec:** Loto-Québec's Mise-o-jeu only
- **Alberta:** PlayAlberta.ca only (regulatory licensing for private operators expected early 2026)
- **Other provinces:** Provincial lottery corporations only

**Offshore Status:** Using offshore betting sites is a legal grey area for Canadian consumers. The Criminal Code focuses on operators, not individual bettors, and no Canadian has been prosecuted for placing bets with offshore operators. However, you have no regulatory recourse if disputes arise.

### Do I have to pay taxes on betting winnings in Canada?

**No, recreational gambling winnings are NOT taxable in Canada.**

Key points:
- Winnings are considered "windfall" income, not taxable employment or business income

**Recommendation:** Keep records of wins and losses anyway, as banks may inquire about large deposits.

## Responsible Gambling in Canada

All reputable sportsbooks offer these tools:
- **Deposit limits:** Daily, weekly, or monthly caps
- **Time limits:** Session duration alerts
- **Reality checks:** Pop-up reminders during extended sessions
- **Self-exclusion:** Temporary or permanent account suspension
- **Cooling-off periods:** Short breaks from betting
