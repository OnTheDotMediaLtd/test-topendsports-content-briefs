**3. Can I bet on World Cup games live?**
Target: "world cup live betting" (150/mo)
- Cover: Yes, all major US sportsbooks offer live in-play betting during World Cup matches. Available markets include next goal scorer, match result, total goals, corners, and cards. Odds update in real-time based on match action. DraftKings and bet365 offer the fastest live odds.

**4. What types of bets can I place on the World Cup?**
Target: "world cup prop bets" (90/mo)
- Cover: Moneyline (match winner including draw), goal spreads, totals (over/under), futures (tournament winner), player props (goals, assists, cards), match props (exact score, first goal), and same-game parlays combining multiple markets within one match.

**5. How do World Cup parlays work?**
Target: "world cup parlay" (60/mo)
- Cover: Combine multiple World Cup selections into one bet for higher payouts. All selections must win for the parlay to pay. Same-game parlays combine bets within one match. Multi-match parlays span different games. Use our parlay calculator to estimate payouts before placing your bet.

**6. What are the current World Cup 2026 odds?**
Target: "world cup odds 2026" (800/mo)
- Cover: Futures odds are available now at all major sportsbooks. Typical favorites include Brazil, Argentina, France, and England. Host nations (USA, Canada, Mexico) often carry value odds. Odds will shift as the tournament approaches and teams announce final squads. Check our World Cup futures page for live updates.

**7. Is World Cup spread betting available in the US?**
Target: "world cup spread betting" (20/mo)
- Cover: Yes, US sportsbooks offer goal spreads (handicap betting) for World Cup matches. A spread of -1.5 means the team must win by 2 or more goals. Alternate spreads and Asian handicaps are also available. Spread betting adds value when one team is a heavy favorite.

**8. What World Cup betting strategies should beginners use?**
Target: "world cup betting strategy" (low vol)
- Cover: Start with small stakes (1-3% of bankroll per bet), focus on group stage matches which are more predictable, research team form and injury reports, consider the draw (common in World Cup group stages), compare odds across multiple sportsbooks, and avoid emotional betting on the home team.
